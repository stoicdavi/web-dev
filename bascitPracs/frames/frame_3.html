Barack Obama

Barack Obama is an American politician and attorney who served as the 44th president of the United States from 2009 to 2017. He was the first African American to hold the office of president.

Early Life and Education

Obama was born in Honolulu, Hawaii in 1961. His father was from Kenya and his mother was from Kansas. He attended Occidental College in Los Angeles for two years before transferring to Columbia University in New York City, where he earned a degree in political science.

Political Career

Obama began his political career as a community organizer in Chicago. He later served in the Illinois State Senate from 1997 to 2004, and then as a United States Senator from Illinois from 2005 to 2008.

Presidency

Obama was elected president in 2008, defeating Republican nominee John McCain. During his presidency, he signed into law the Affordable Care Act, also known as Obamacare, which aimed to provide affordable health insurance to all Americans. He also oversaw the end of the war in Iraq and the killing of Osama bin Laden, the leader of al-Qaeda.

Post-Presidency

Since leaving office, Obama has remained active in public life. He has written several books, including his memoir "A Promised Land," and has been involved in various philanthropic and political initiatives.
